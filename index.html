US Real Estate Price Growth

10-year trend showing stable growth in property values

US Average Price Growth

Average prices rose from roughly 350 to nearly 690 between Q1 2015 and Q1 2025.

This reflects an approximate increase of 97% nationwide over 10 years, highlighting real estate's strength as a long-term asset class.

Growth accelerated sharply between 2020 and 2022, correlating with pandemic-era housing shifts and historically low interest rates.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)

Florida Price Growth

Florida's market outpaced the national average, climbing from about 320 to over 800 during the same period — a remarkable increase of roughly 150%.

The state saw rapid price escalation from 2020 to 2022, driven by migration trends and high demand for sunbelt properties.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)

Arizona Price Growth

Arizona has seen strong appreciation, with average prices rising from around 320 to nearly 710 between 2015 and 2025 — a gain of approximately 122%.

A sharp upswing occurred between late 2020 and early 2022, fueled by population inflows and tech-sector expansion.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)
