U.S. Real Estate Price Growth

10-year trend analysis demonstrating consistent property value appreciation

U.S. Average Price Growth

Average prices increased from approximately $350,000 to nearly $690,000 between Q1 2015 and Q1 2025.

This represents an appreciation of approximately 97% nationwide over the decade, highlighting real estate's strength as a capital asset class.

Accelerated growth occurred between 2020 and 2022, correlating with pandemic-era housing dynamics and historically low interest rates.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)

Florida Price Growth

Florida's market exceeded national averages, appreciating from approximately $320,000 to over $800,000 during the same period—a remarkable increase of approximately 150%.

The state experienced rapid price acceleration from 2020 to 2022, driven by demographic shifts and heightened demand for sunbelt properties.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)

Arizona Price Growth

Arizona demonstrated robust appreciation, with average prices rising from approximately $320,000 to nearly $710,000 between 2015 and 2025—representing a gain of approximately 122%.

A significant upswing occurred between late 2020 and early 2022, fueled by population inflows and technology sector expansion.

Official Source: Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED)
